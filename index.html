Despite the recent increase in concussion awareness, 50% of concussions remain undiagnosed for several weeks, placing patients at further risk of permanent brain damage. Locaze is a mobile application that uses eye tracking technology to assess concussion risk in athletes, providing users with accessible, time-efficient, and comprehensive results.

Assess Concussion Risk in 3 Easy Steps

Accessible

The concussion assessment can be completed on a variety of different platforms.

Time Efficient

Locaze's complete concussion assessment can be completed in under five minutes, providing users with rapid, accurate results. Our assessment is four times faster than leading competitors.

Comprehensive

Users complete a series of 4 clinically validated eye movement tests to assess concussion risk. Comprehensively, these tests account for the most commonly observed oculomotor damages that occur after a concussion.

Our Clinically Validated Oculomotor Tests

Saccades Test

Saccadic function is characterized by the rapid shift of the center of gaze between multiple visual fields. 30% of concussed patients demonstrate saccadic dysfunction. Locaze tests saccadic eye movements by prompting users to use their eyes to track a dot that appears in different horizontal, vertical, and diagonal patterns. The time taken to locate the dot will be compared to the baseline.

Smooth Pursuit Eye Movements Test

Smooth pursuit function is characterized by continuously tracking a moving target without disruption. Current data suggests that concussed patients often demonstrate impaired visual tracking capabilities. Locaze tests for smooth pursuit eye movements by prompting users to track a moving red dot as it varies its speed. The percent of time that the gaze diverges from the predicted path will be compared to the baseline.

Accommodation Test

Accommodation is characterized by the shift in eye focus to maintain a clear image of an object as it varies its distance from the observer. 51% of concussed patients demonstrate accommodative dysfunction. Locaze tests accommodation with a similar test to convergence, except the user will be prompted to close one eye. The distance at which the image is no longer clear will be compared to the baseline.

Vestibular-Ocular Reflex Test

Vestibular-ocular reflex (VOR) stabilizes gaze by moving the eyes in response to head movement. Current data suggests VOR abnormalities are present in over 50% of mild traumatic brain injury patients.
